# Branding Options — Elite Mobile Car Detailing

**Status / decision (DECIDED 2026-06-19):** **Option B** — keep **"Elite Mobile Car Detailing"**
and pair it with the tagline **"No hookups. No hassle. Just shine."** (the punchy, differentiator-led
candidate below). A more ownable rebrand (Option C) stays captured here for later, but is NOT
happening for launch. Rationale unchanged: a rebrand is cheapest to do *before* you've built up
Google reviews, citations, and printed materials — revisit only if you later lean toward a
distinctive name. The tagline appears as the brand wordmark line (header + footer), in OG/meta,
and as the JSON-LD `slogan` (see `INITIAL_IDEA.md` §8 and `SEO_STRATEGY.md` §A4).

## The tradeoff in one line

A **descriptive** name ("Elite Mobile Car Detailing") helps a little for SEO and instantly
says what you do — but it's generic, crowded ("Elite" detailers are everywhere), and nearly
impossible to trademark or own. A **distinctive** name builds long-term equity and is
ownable/defensible, but means redoing the logo, Google Business Profile, domain, and any
citations. Note: Google ranks the Map Pack on your *category* + reviews + proximity, **not**
on keywords stuffed into the business name — keyword-stuffing the GBP title actually violates
guidelines and risks suspension. So the SEO upside of a descriptive name is small.

---

## Option A — Keep "Elite Mobile Car Detailing"
- **Pros:** zero rework; already (presumably) on the GBP; descriptive.
- **Cons:** generic, hard to own/trademark, blends in with competitors.
- **If you keep it:** pair it with a memorable tagline (below) to add distinctiveness.

## Option B — Keep the name + add a tagline/wordmark  ✅ CHOSEN
Lowest-effort way to stand out without a full rebrand. Tagline candidates:

- **"Showroom shine, delivered to your driveway."**  *(matches the hero H1; premium + benefit)*
- **"We bring the water, the power, and the shine."**  *(sells the self-contained USP)*
- ✅ **"No hookups. No hassle. Just shine."**  *(punchy; leans on the differentiator)* — **CHOSEN (2026-06-19)**
- **"The detail shop that drives to you."**  *(plain-spoken, memorable)*
- **"Fully self-contained. Booked around you."**  *(USP + convenience)*

## Option C — Rebrand to something more ownable
Distinctive names with rationale (check availability before committing — see below):

- **Driveway Shine Co.** — ties straight to "delivered to your driveway"; friendly, brandable, easy to say.
- **Showroom Mobile Detailing** — leans on the "showroom shine" promise; premium tone, still legible in search.
- **Lone Star Mobile Detail** — Texas identity; locally distinctive across DFW.
- **Northstar Mobile Detail** — nods to Richardson / north Dallas; clean, modern, ownable.

*(Bonus directions if none land: "Rig & Shine," "Driveline Detailing," "Caliber Mobile Detail.")*

## Before committing to a rebrand — quick checks
1. **Domain** available (`.com` ideally) — check a registrar.
2. **Trademark** — search the USPTO TESS database for conflicts in the auto-services class.
3. **Social handles** + **GBP name** available/consistent.
4. **DFW competitor clash** — Google the name + "detailing Dallas" to avoid confusion.
5. **Cost of switching** — if you already have reviews/citations under "Elite," factor the
   migration (GBP rename keeps reviews, but citations + printed assets must be updated for NAP
   consistency — see `SEO_STRATEGY.md` §B3).
